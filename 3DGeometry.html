Coordinates of a Point in 3D: \( P(x, y, z) \)
Distance Between Two Points: \( AB = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2 + (z_2 - z_1)^2} \)
Section Formula (Internal Division): If point \( P \) divides \( AB \) in ratio \( m:n \), then \( P = \left( \frac{mx_2 + nx_1}{m + n}, \frac{my_2 + ny_1}{m + n}, \frac{mz_2 + nz_1}{m + n} \right) \)
Section Formula (External Division): \( P = \left( \frac{mx_2 - nx_1}{m - n}, \frac{my_2 - ny_1}{m - n}, \frac{mz_2 - nz_1}{m - n} \right) \)
Midpoint Formula: \( M = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2}, \frac{z_1 + z_2}{2} \right) \)
Centroid of a Triangle: For vertices \( A(x_1, y_1, z_1), B(x_2, y_2, z_2), C(x_3, y_3, z_3) \), \( G = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2 + x_3}{3}, \frac{y_1 + y_2 + y_3}{3}, \frac{z_1 + z_2 + z_3}{3} \right) \)
