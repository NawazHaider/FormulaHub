Arithmetic Progression (AP): \( a, a + d, a + 2d, \dots \)
\( n^\text{th} \text{ term: } a_n = a + (n - 1)d \)
\( \text{Sum of first } n \text{ terms: } S_n = \frac{n}{2} [2a + (n - 1)d] \)
Geometric Progression (GP): \( a, ar, ar^2, \dots \)
\( n^\text{th} \text{ term: } a_n = ar^{n - 1} \)
\( \text{Sum of first } n \text{ terms: } S_n = a \frac{1 - r^n}{1 - r} \quad (r \ne 1) \)
\( \text{Sum to infinity: } S_\infty = \frac{a}{1 - r} \quad (|r| < 1) \)
Special Series:
  • \( \sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n + 1)}{2} \)
  • \( \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n + 1)(2n + 1)}{6} \)
  • \( \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^3 = \left[\frac{n(n + 1)}{2}\right]^2 \)
Miscellaneous:
  • If \( S_n \) is the sum of the first \( n \) terms, then \( a_n = S_n - S_{n - 1} \)
