Chapter 14: Probability – Formula Sheet

Probability of an Event: \( P(E) = \frac{\text{Number of favorable outcomes}}{\text{Total number of outcomes}} \)
Range of Probability: \( 0 \leq P(E) \leq 1 \)
Complement of an Event: \( P(E') = 1 - P(E) \)
Addition Theorem:
  • If \( A \) and \( B \) are two events: \( P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) \)
  • If \( A \) and \( B \) are mutually exclusive: \( P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) \)
Conditional Probability: \( P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} \), provided \( P(B) \ne 0 \)
Independent Events: \( P(A \cap B) = P(A) \cdot P(B) \)
Total Probability Theorem: If \( B_1, B_2, \dots, B_n \) are mutually exclusive and exhaustive events: \( P(A) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} P(B_i) \cdot P(A|B_i) \)
Bayes’ Theorem: \( P(B_i|A) = \frac{P(B_i) \cdot P(A|B_i)}{\sum_{j=1}^{n} P(B_j) \cdot P(A|B_j)} \)
