Statements: A sentence that is either true or false but not both.
Negation: If statement is \( p \), then its negation is \( \sim p \)
Compound Statements:
  • Conjunction: \( p \land q \) (true only if both are true)
  • Disjunction: \( p \lor q \) (true if at least one is true)
Implication: \( p \Rightarrow q \) means "if \( p \), then \( q \)"
Contrapositive: \( \sim q \Rightarrow \sim p \)
Converse: \( q \Rightarrow p \)
Inverse: \( \sim p \Rightarrow \sim q \)
Bi-conditional Statement: \( p \Leftrightarrow q \) means "\( p \) if and only if \( q \)"
Truth Table Basics:
  • \( p \land q \): true only when both \( p \) and \( q \) are true
  • \( p \lor q \): false only when both \( p \) and \( q \) are false
  • \( \sim p \): opposite truth value of \( p \)
Valid Argument Forms:
  • Modus Ponens: If \( p \Rightarrow q \) and \( p \) is true, then \( q \) is true
  • Modus Tollens: If \( p \Rightarrow q \) and \( q \) is false, then \( p \) is false
